Frontløberne - Creative Charge

Project info

Date: May 2021

Duration: 4 weeks

Team: Group of 5 students

Tools

Keywords

Scrum
Target Audience Analysis
Personas
Sitemap
Tone of Voice
Mockups

Client

Our client was Frontløberne - an open creative space for young culture creators and entrepreneurs.
It has a rich history of organizing events and embarking projects, influencing the young Aarhus community.

Case

How can our team help Frontløberne with a digital solution that raises awareness of the impact that Frontløberne has on the creative community in Aarhus, reflects their offline presence accurately and attracts new members?

Process

To kick off our project, we started with the research and analysis of our client and target audience by conducting interviews and desk research. We discovered our main target group - young adults aged between 25-34 years that are creative and love cultural activities - and created our Personas.


After that we started brainstorming on the content and structure of the solution needed to meed the user needs and business objectives. We created a Sitemap - hierarchy of our solution’s information architecture. Then we established the Tone of Voice to guide how the content should be written.


After setting the direction of our project’s design, getting inspiration and brainstorming on design elements of our solution, we carried out the design process of creating a Mood board, Style tile, Wireframes and Mockups. Lastly, we made a Design guide for anyone to use when creating future content.

Mood Board

Style Tile

Mockups

Reflection

Based on our research, we decided on creating a brand-new website for their cultural center. We proceeded to change the old design for a better user experience and new audience.

Our solution is of great importance for the non-members because it introduces them to what Frontløberne is about as a cultural organization and it inspires them to apply. Besides that, it helps current members to keep a track on information such as events and portfolios.

Dormbuddy app

Project info

Date: April 2021

Duration: 2 weeks

Team: Group of 5 students

Tools

Keywords

UX/UI
Prototyping
Project Management
Visual Identity
API
Usability Test

Client

We created a solution for Skejbo student dormitory located in Aarhus.

Case

Only a few dorms in Aarhus have their own bespoke visual identity.
The goal of the project was to develop an interactive user experience that would be benefit the dorm and its' inhabitants.

Process

The project started with throughout desk and field research on the client and target audience. We conducted several interviews to get an idea of who lived in the dorms and what problems they were facing.

Our findings showed that there is a lack of information about Skejbo kollegiet, therefore a resident could easily feel confused.

After analysing our research data, we discovered our target group - mostly Danish but also international Skejbo residents between 18-25 years that often struggle to find proper information about the dorm. We created two Personas that represent user needs.

When it comes to the design, we needed to establish the visual identity of Skejbo. We made a Creative Brief and summarized all of the technical purposes and visual goals for the solution.

To sum up, while the solution should be perceived as a resident guide on life at the dorm which is UX-friendly, informative, visually descriptive and properly organized, we wanted our design to associate with friendliness, simplicity and youth.

Our design process continued with implementing the Mockups. Based on user needs, we decided that our app should contain 5 navigational pages - dashboard with all the content shortened, news and events page, marketplace, assistance page and map.

We tried to implement decluttering to preserve the user with only what they need to know and keep interface elements to a minimum. We also used familiar and intuitive screens that do not require additional explanation, for example, the onboarding screens educate the user about the functions and benefits of the app.

Reflection

We believe that we created something that would be useful on a day to day basis for the Skejbo residents. Current situation of the information online is all over the place and our solution organises and puts it in easy navigable space.

Since we weren’t to develop an actual mobile app, we developed a prototype using HTML, CSS and JS with a mobile-first approach. We used the mapbox’s API to display nearby places relevant to the people living in the dorm. The API allowed us to create custom pin points and adjust the colours to fit our UI.

Selfly - self-care app

Project info

Date: October 2021

Duration: 3 weeks

Team: Group of 4 students

Tools

Keywords

UX/UI
User Centered Development
Headless CMS
SPA Architecture
Usability Test

Case

Developing a concept for a web app and implementing an interactive prototype with JavaScript, HTML, and CSS.

Concept

It is only natural to have feelings change throughout the day and experience different emotions - it is normal to feel stressed, frustrated or unmotivated sometimes. But what is really important is how we cope with those emotional burdens.

Many times people lack ideas of how they can take care of themselves and with the ocean of content out there it can be very hard to find something that is right for them. This is where Selfly comes to the rescue. Selfly was born from the idea of helping people find new ways to boost their well-being.

Process

In order to create a well-designed solution that meets the target audience’s needs, the research process was a very important foundation for our next decisions.

When we had just picked the concept for our app, we had a lot of broad ideas of what topics we could touch on. After conducting desk research by searching for relevant statistics about people indulging in self-care, we were able to determine our target group - young people aged between 16-35 who tend to experience emotional burdens.

But to further understand the target audience and identify their goals, we mapped the User Scenario and User Journey - concise representations of user experience.
We mapped the “steps” and feelings that the user goes through before, during and after using our app. This really helped us to get into our user's shoes and look at what they wish to achieve, so that we can reflect on it when arguing for our design choices.

In our Style Tile we gathered all the UI elements like font, colors and icons that we should use in our design.

We want users to feel at ease when using our app. Sometimes it is hard for people to express their feelings, so we want their first impression of the app to convey trust, friendliness and caring.

The illustrations from the home screen are supposed to represent the user's emotions, but if that’s too overwhelming for them, there is also the quiz – a simplified version which is a step-by-step process that gets you through different screens with more concrete emotion representation.

The app’s general design reflects softness and positive vibes, due to the silky-pastel color palette and roundness, which distresses and alleviates the user.

We created our HI-FI prototype, in other words Mockups, out of tested Wireframes and by reflecting on our Style Tile to keep our design elements consistent. All of the content is structured in a way to meet the user goals and help to achieve the tasks that the users might want to perform on the app.

To kick-off our development process, we set up a Github repository to ensure easier collaboration.

As our data source, we use Headless Wordpress where we created our activities posts and assigned categories (moods) and tags (time) to them. We developed our solution in an SPA architecture - single HTML page with dynamic content load and change.

Reflection

With Selfly you aren't given a universal thing to help you deal with your situation. But rather you get tailored recommendations which would help you build up your own healthy way to deal with different emotional states and possibly create healthy coping mechanisms.

UX Challenge - Swibbid

Project info

Date: March 2021

Duration: 3 days

Team: Group of 4 students

Tools

Keywords

UX/UI
Sketching
Storyboard
Prototyping
Usability Test

Client

Our client was Swibbid - a marketplace for buying and selling used items.

Case

I worked on this project during the UX Challenge organized by Danish Design Centre. During a 3 day period me and my team built a prototype for Swibbid. Swibbid has already found a way to list 1 item for sale fast and easy, but they had one important task for us:

How can a user sell multiple items fast under one listing, while still keeping the announcement informative for buyers?

Process

In the beginning of the Challenge, we had a meeting with Swibbid team. After introduction to their app main target group - women 30+, we started brainstorming on the possible solutions. Throughout the process, we used a white board tool called Miro.

On the first day, we started with “How Might We” questions to get the inspiration and ideas running. Each of us worked on their own HMW's and later together we identified the most important one - How might we come up with a framework for multiple items' description?

After setting our design goals, we moved to the sketching phase - each of us delivered 3 ideas of how the solution might look like. We had a speed critique and voted on the best features to merge them into our solution.

Our second day started with creating a Storyboard - an enlarged and robust version of the sketch, describing the interaction between the user and the product.

Based on the storyboard, we created our first prototype and tested it on 2 users. We got a lot of useful feedback that we used for improvements.

Reflection

The final phase of the UX Challenge was to get ready for the presentation of the results - both to the companies and to the plenary event. The Swibbid team was pleased with our solution, as during our process we gathered a lot of data and ideas on their app improvements.

nuuii - Web Documentary

Project info

Date: February 2021

Duration: 3 weeks

Team: Group of 5 students

Tools

Keywords

Storytelling
Creative Brief
Quantitative research
Personas
Target group analysis

Client

We worked on this project for a company called nuuii - a company that is selling sustainable cleaning products.

Case

The goal of the project was to create a web documentary - a story-telling website for a client.

Process

To first generate ideas about the web documentary, we conducted a desk research on the client and market they are in.
We found out that one of the ways how nuuii are more sustainable and different from their competitors is that the production of their products and the products themselves don't consume any water. That makes it easier to transport the products and leads to reducing the amount of CO2.

We therefore decided to use the reduction of water consumption as our main storyline for the web documentary. The web documentary was made in a way to attract and educate consumers using video, illustrations and short texts, rather than them having to read long paragraphs.

We performed a thorough target audience analysis by demographic, geographic, psychographic and behavioural variables that helped us to establish a main target group - 25-35 year old women and men that reside in Denmark and are interested in being environmentally-friendly. We then created our Personas - displaying both user needs and business objectives.

When we started on our creative process, we made a Creative Brief - the representation of the design and content that the solution should contain.

In Creative Brief, we also stated that the goal of our project was to create a non-linear storytelling website that tells one of the nuuii’s sustainability stories – the importance of environmental impact that nuuii’s cleaning products do not contain water. This is when we got an idea to prioritise the storytelling layout based on the Golden Circle of the company.

So, when we were creating Wireframe for the website, we structured the content according to nuuii’s Golden Circle:

1. Content that explains nuuii’s WHY - general sustainability facts, cleaning product and water consumption statistics.
2. nuuii’s HOW - video about company, nuuii’s story and values.
3. nuuii's WHAT - product gallery and information.

We think that this Golden Circle interpretation on the website helps to communicate with the user because, based on Golden Circle’s creator’s Simon Sinek theory, it inspires and influences the user's behaviour.

When we started choosing content and designing, we wanted to make sure that our solution fits nuuii’s concept. We made a Mood board with nuuii’s color palette and pictures that inspire sustainability to represent nuuii’s aesthetics.

We then created a range of illustrations to make our solution more attention grabbing and finalized our Mockups.

Reflection

I think that our web documentary creates several values for our client:

1. Because of many illustratrations and storytelling layout, it grabs the user’s attention, therefore motivating the user to become more environmentally friendly and tempted to buy nuuii products.
2. It also provides a stronger emphasis on the importance of reducing water consumption and CO2 because of numerous sustainability facts which leads the user to understand why being environmentally friendly is important for creating a better place for future generations.

And for the user this solution is a more engaging method of learning about the importance of the reduction of water consumption in nuuii products rather than having to read it as long text.
